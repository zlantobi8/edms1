About EMDMS

Examination Malpractice Detection & Management System — a Computer-Based Test platform built to run entirely on a local network, purpose-built for institutions like ours.

Why this exists

Paper-based examinations are slow to grade, easy to leak, and hard to supervise at scale. Cloud-based CBT platforms solve some of that, but depend on internet connectivity that many exam halls simply don't have — or shouldn't rely on for something as sensitive as an examination. EMDMS was built to close that gap: a full computer-based testing system that runs entirely on a local server in the exam hall, with no internet connection required at any point during the exam.

What it does

EMDMS gives administrators a complete toolkit to manage the academic structure, register students and invigilators, build or import exam questions, and publish exams on a schedule. Students take exams through a guided, timed interface with autosave built in. Invigilators get a live view of every student's webcam feed alongside real-time progress, with the ability to warn, lock, or end a student's session directly from the dashboard. Every webcam feed is also recorded automatically, so nothing is missed even if it wasn't caught in the moment.

Built for the exam hall

Every design decision in EMDMS starts from the same constraint: it has to work reliably in a room full of students on a local network, without depending on anything outside that room. That means no cloud storage, no third-party APIs, and careful attention to keeping the server lightweight even when dozens of students are connected and recording at once.
